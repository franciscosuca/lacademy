## 2. Safety

### 2.1 Intended Use
Tank bottom valves of type H_A/T are stroke valves and are used for controlled opening and closing tank outlets. The valve is monitored, controlled and operated by the operator's installation.

> **CRITICAL NOTICE:** Pressure hammers and excessive control air pressures can destroy the bellows. Therefore, the control air pressure should not exceed the maximum value according to the technical data.

*Hint! The manufacturer will not accept any liability for damage resulting from any use of the valve which is not in accordance with the designated use of the valve. The risk is borne solely by the operating company.*

#### 2.1.1 Requirements for Operation
The prerequisite for reliable and safe operation of the component is proper transportation and storage as well as professional installation and assembly. Operating the unit within the limits of its designated use also involves adhering to the operating, inspection and maintenance instructions.

#### 2.1.2 Pressure Equipment Directive
* The component is a piece of pressure equipment (without safety function) in the sense of the pressure equipment directive 2014/68/EU. Classified according to Annex II in category 1.
* According to the scope of directive 2014/34/EC, article 1, paragraph 2, f, the exception of the directive applies, due to conformity with the machine directive 2006/42/EU.
* Nominal diameters smaller than DN 25 are subject to article 4, paragraph 3 of the Pressure Equipment Directive which specifies sound engineering practice.
* Nominal diameters $\ge$ IPS 4“; DN 125 valid for the fluid group II.
* In the event of any deviations, GEA Tuchenhagen GmbH will supply a specific Declaration of Conformity.

#### 2.1.3 Use in Potentially Explosive Atmospheres (ATEX)
In areas with an explosive atmosphere, only valves suitable for use in such areas may be used. Refer to and observe the additional instruction manual “Hygienic valves ATEX models”. For details regarding the marking of valves for use in potentially explosive areas also refer to the additional instruction manual “Hygienic valves ATEX models”. If these valves are used in areas with a potentially explosive atmosphere, you must absolutely comply with the requirements of directive 2014/34/EC with respect to all ignition hazards.

#### 2.1.4 Improper Operating Conditions
The operational safety of the component cannot be guaranteed under improper operating conditions. Therefore avoid improper operating conditions.
The operation of the component is not permitted if:
* Persons or objects are in the danger zone.
* Safety devices are not working or were removed.
* Malfunctions have been detected on the component.
* Damage to the component has been detected.
* Maintenance intervals have been exceeded.

### 2.8 Residual Dangers and Measures
* **Danger to life (Inadvertent switch-on of the valve):** Effectively disconnect all components, effectively prevent switch-on. Observe the 5 safety rules: (1) Isolate from the power supply, (2) Take appropriate measures to prevent switch on, (3) Test absence of voltage, (4) Earthing and short-circuiting, (5) Cover or safeguard any adjacent live parts.
* **Spring tension in the actuator:** For large valves: Danger to life caused by the pressurized spring in the actuator. Do not open the actuator, rather return it to GEA Tuchenhagen for proper disposal.
* **Risk of injury (Moving or sharp-edged parts):** The operator must exercise caution and prudence. Wear suitable work clothing, protective gloves, and safety shoes. * In the event of malfunctions, shut down the valve (disconnect from the power and air supply) and secure it against being used.

## 4. Transport and Storage

### 4.1 Storage Conditions
The valves, valve inserts or spare parts should be stored in a dry place, free of vibrations and dust, and protected from light. To avoid damage, leave the components in their original packaging if possible.
If, during transport or storage, the valve is going to be exposed to temperatures $\le 0^\circ	ext{C}$, it must be dried beforehand and suitable measures must be taken to protect it from damage.

*Hint! We recommend that the valve should be stored at a temperature of $\ge 5^\circ	ext{C}$ for a period of 24 hours prior to any handling (disassembling the housings / activation of actuators) so that any ice crystals formed by condensation water can melt.*

### 4.2 Transport
* Only use suitable hoist and slings for transporting the package units/valves.
* Observe the pictograms on the package.
* Handle valves with care to avoid damage caused by impact or careless loading and unloading. The outside synthetic materials are susceptible to breaking.
* Control tops must be protected from animal and vegetable fats.
* Only allow qualified staff to transport the valve.
* Movable parts must be properly secured.
* Under no circumstances should anyone stand under a suspended load.
* Take care when transporting the valve. Do not grip sensitive parts of the unit to lift or push the unit or to support yourself. Avoid jerky movements when putting down the unit.

#### 4.2.1 Scope of Supply
After taking delivery of the component, check if:
* The details on the type plate correspond to the data in the order and delivery documents.
* The equipment is complete and all components are in good order.

### 8.2 Notes on Installation
The valve is installed under the tank. Care must be taken to ensure that the valve housing and the pipe system can drain properly.
To prevent damage, make sure that:
* The valve is installed between the tank and the pipe system free of tension.
* No foreign materials (e.g. tools, bolts, lubricants) are left in the system.

### 8.3 Control Head
If external valves are connected in a control top with several solenoid valves, make sure that the control air pressure in the main actuator does not fall below the operating pressure.

### 8.4 Valve with Detachable Pipe Connection Elements
1. **Caution! Liquids in pipes:** Danger of injury due to liquid spraying out. Before releasing any pipe or hinged clamp connections: drain and, if necessary, clean or flush the pipe.
2. Separate the pipe section in which the valve is to be fitted from the rest of the piping system to prevent product entering again.
3. **Notice:** Seals are wearing parts. Old seals will cause malfunction of the valve. When fitting the valve be sure to carry out a visual check of the condition of the sealing surface.
4. **Step:** Fit valves with detachable pipe connection elements – using suitable connection fittings – directly into the pipe system.

### 8.5 Weld Valve with Housing Connection (H_A/T/F)

> **WARNING! SPRING TENSION IN THE VALVE:** Danger of injury when detaching the cap nut at the housing as the released spring pretension will suddenly lift the actuator. Therefore, release the spring tension before detaching the cap nut by supplying the actuator with compressed air at max. 8 bar.

*Notice: Welding distortions. An open housing can warp during welding. To avoid welding distortions, always seal the housing before welding.*

#### Welding Housing Connection Process:
If the valves are delivered with screwed in housing connection H_A/T/F (420), first undo the screws (206) and the housing connection (420).
1. Weld the housing connection (420) into the tank bottom.
    * *Hint! Use a welding jig when welding (see spare parts list 229-103.68 / 229-103.70). Pay attention to the welding instructions (WPS) 221RLI004112D / 221RLI009047DE.*

#### Welding the Valve Housing Process:
1. Release the spring tension.
2. Remove the valve insert (see section 10.4).
3. Fit the housing and fix it in several places around the circumference.
4. To avoid welding distortions, always seal the housing before welding.
5. Flush the housing with forming gas from the inside to push the oxygen out of the system.
6. Weld the housing into the pipe system; use welding filler if necessary. When technically possible, use the WIG-orbital welding process with pulse configuration, according to guidelines EHEDG documentation 35.
7. Passivate the seam after welding.
8. Assemble the valve and vent the actuator (the bellows is lowered).

*Hint! Welding method: We recommend using the automatic orbital welding method. All welding work should only be performed by certified welders or machine operators (orbital welders).*

### 8.6 Welding the Valve Housing (H_A/T standard)
1. Release the spring tension by supplying compressed air at max. 8 bar.
2. Remove the valve insert.
3. Fit the housing (391) into the tank bottom and fix it in several places around the circumference. Pay attention to welding instructions (WPS) 221RLI004129D.
4. Always seal the housing before welding to avoid warping.
5. Flush the housing with forming gas from the inside.
6. Weld the housing into the pipe system using WIG-orbital welding with pulse configuration.
7. Passivate the seam after welding.
8. Assemble the valve and vent the actuator.

## 9. Cleaning

### 9.1 CIP Cleaning Guidelines
All parts in contact with product must be cleaned at regular intervals. Always observe the safety data sheets issued by the cleaning agent manufacturers. Only use cleaning agents which do not cause damage to the seals and the inner parts of the valve. When the pipe is cleaned, the cleaning medium also flows through and cleans the valve housings.
Method and parameters should be determined and defined by the operator in accordance with the relevant process and product. The cleaning effect must be checked regularly by the operator.

### 9.2 Passivation
Before commissioning a plant, passivation is commonly carried out for long pipes and tanks. Valve blocks are usually excepted from this. Passivation is typically performed using nitric acid ($	ext{HNO}_3$) at approx. $80^\circ	ext{C}$ ($176^\circ	ext{F}$) at a concentration of 3% and a contact time of 6 to 8 hours.

### 9.3 Sterilization (SIP)
The valve is suitable for SIP sterilization (sterilization in place).

*Hint! VESTA® bellows achieve optimal sealing only after an initial SIP cleaning.*
* **Medium:** Saturated steam
* **Temperature:** $135^\circ	ext{C}$ ($275^\circ	ext{F}$)
* **Switching:** Clockwise during the SIP process.
* **Requirement:** The valve must be brought to the closed position for a brief period (min. 3 seconds) immediately after SIP cleaning. During commissioning, regularly check all sealing points for leaks. Exchange defective seals and repeat the SIP process.

2. Drain all pipe system elements that lead to the valve and, if necessary, clean or rinse them.
3. Disconnect the power supply.
4. Take the valve out of the pipe section, with all housings and housing connections if possible.

#### 10.4.2 Removing the Valve Insert

> **WARNING! SPRING TENSION IN THE VALVE (NC Variant):** Danger of injury when detaching the cap nut (252) at the housing as the released spring pretension will suddenly lift the actuator. Therefore, release the spring tension before detaching the cap nut by supplying the actuator with compressed air at max. 8 bar.

*Notice: The potentiometer spindle (P) is a sensitive component. Damage to potentiometer spindle (P) can occur. Handle carefully. Do not remove the hood (H) of the control top (B). Do not disassemble the attachments 1 and 2 of the control top for air/air actuators.*

* **Steps for Spring-closing Valve NC:**
    1. Remove electrical and pneumatic connections from the control top (B).
    2. Vent actuator at connection (117) with compressed air, max. 8 bar. The bellows (15) are raised.
    3. Undo the cap nut (252) using the joint spanner and remove the valve insert. Do not hit the valve parts against the housing.
    4. Interrupt the compressed air supply. The bellows (15) are lowered.
* **Steps for Spring-opening Valve NO:**
    1. Remove electrical and pneumatic connections from the control top (B).
    2. Undo the cap nut (252) using the joint spanner and remove the valve insert. Do not hit the valve parts against the housing.

#### 10.4.3 Removing the Bellows
* **Warning! Spring tension in spring-opening valve (NO):** Danger of injury. Do not put your hand into the valve housing. Before removing the valve insert, pressurize connection (22) with air.
* **Warning! Risk of crushing/clamping:** In the case of a removed valve insert (NC and NO), there is a risk of injury at point (16). Do not reach into the gap at point (16)!
* **Step:** Unscrew bellows (15) from the valve rod thread.

#### 10.4.4 Disassembling the Visual Position Indicator
1. Unscrew the position indicator (257) using tools.
2. Take the O-ring (256) out of the housing (138).
3. Remove spacer (212) and screw (258).

#### 10.4.5 Disassembling the Pneumatic Actuator
* **Hint!** For valves in stainless steel, unscrew the screw-in slip connections (117) (hexagon socket AF 2.5).
* **Removing Actuator NC (Spring-closing):**
    1. Unscrew cover (138) with screwdriver (hexagon socket) AF17.
    2. Take the O-ring (118) out of the housing (138).
    3. Remove the compression spring (18), undo the hexagon nut (10) using a socket wrench insert and slide off the rod (139) with the washer (98).
    4. Pull the rod (139) down and out of the lantern (9).
    5. Push the piston (228) with a screwdriver up and out of the lantern, also remove the washer (98).
    6. Remove the O-rings (226, 6, 186).
    * *Note on Stainless Steel Version:* The anti-twist protection can be removed to replace the O-ring (29). To this end, push the pin (251) inwards (the rod must already be removed for this). Remove the sleeve (219) downwards and remove the O-ring (29).

    4. Pull the rod (139) down and out of the lantern (9).
    5. Remove the piston (228) together with the pressure spring (18) and the washer (98).
    6. Remove the O-rings (226, 6, 186).

#### 10.4.6 Removing the Control Top T.VIS® V-1/P-1
* **Notice:** The potentiometer spindle (P) is a sensitive component. Handle it carefully.
* **Steps:**
    1. Remove electrical and pneumatic connections from the control top (B). Do not remove the hood (H) of the control top (B). Do not disassemble the attachments 1 and 2 for control tops for air/air actuators.
    2. Turn the control top (B) to the left (in the direction of the arrow) until the stop is reached. Pull upward to remove.

#### 10.4.7 Removing the Manual Actuator
1. Remove the round plug (259), unscrew the hexagon nut (217) AF13 together with the washer (212).
2. Unscrew the handwheel (82), remove the spring package (53) upwards, pull out rod (139) downwards.
3. Remove the O-ring (186).

### 10.5 Maintenance & Preparation Cleaning

#### 10.5.1 General Notes
* **Notice (Precision Areas):** The bellows with valve shaft (15) and housing seat (391) are precision areas. They must not be damaged! Handle with care.
* **Notice (Detergents):** Only use detergents which are non-abrasive and not aggressive towards stainless steel. Observe safety sheets.
* *Hint! With plastic actuators made from PPS, particular attention must be paid to the selection of a suitable cleaning agent. We recommend replacing the PP plastic actuator after 2 years.*

#### 10.5.2 Lubricating Seals and Threads
* **Caution! Damage to seals:** Ensure an adequate film of lubricant is applied. No grease residues must be visible once the valve has been assembled completely. For product contact seals only use suitable greases/oils.
* **Critical Rule:** **Do not grease the bellows and the O-ring behind it.**
* **Procedure:** Lightly grease the valve rod thread. Grease all seals – including the O-rings of the actuator – very thinly.
* *Recommendation:* GEA Tuchenhagen recommends Cassida P1 (Material No. 413-134). This lubricant is food grade and has NSF-H1 registration. Running dry must be avoided!

4. Push the second washer (98) from the top onto the rod (139). Tighten the hexagon nut (10) using a socket wrench insert. This loads the pressure spring (18).

## 11. Alarms & Troubleshooting

> **NOTICE (Risk of Product Loss):** Ignoring malfunctions may cause considerable damage to property and loss of product. In the worst case, it can result in a loss of sterility in the process. Make sure that malfunctions are quickly identified and fixed.

| Fault | Possible Cause | Remedy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Valve does not work** | Fault in system configuration/controller.<br>No compressed air or air pressure too low.<br>Fault in the electrical system.<br>Actuator leaking. | Check system configuration.<br>Check air supply and filter. Check hoses for leaks.<br>Check external controller & electrical lines.<br>Replace actuator seals. |
| **Valve does not close tight** | Dirt/foreign material between seat and bellows.<br>Seat area in the housing damaged.<br>PTFE bellows defective. | Clean valve housing and bellows.<br>Replace the housing.<br>Replace PTFE bellows. |
| **Valve closes too slowly** | O-rings in actuator/control top are dry (friction losses). | Grease O-rings using Cassida P1. |
| **Medium leaking from lantern leakage holes** | Bellows are not fitted correctly or the sealing lip at the bellows is damaged. | Fit the bellows correctly or replace if damaged immediately. |
| **Maximum stroke not reached** | Leakage inside the actuator. | Check internal sealing surfaces for damage. Replace the seals. |
| **Valve feedback not correct** | Feedback unit not fitted correctly.<br>Proximity switch out of position or defective.<br>Fault in the power supply. | Check feedback unit fitment.<br>Check proximity switch position and readjust.<br>Check wiring. |
| **Actuator filling with water (Vertical install)**| Unfavorable effect of exterior plant cleaning. | Align air connections downwards or away from cleaning spray direction. |

### 15.2 Step-by-Step Pulse Welding Execution Sequence
* **Prerequisite Constraints:** Welding may only be carried out utilizing a valve block that is directly connected to active streaming forming gas lines (Reference system sheet: 231ELI006854).
1. **Tack Welding Step:** Staple with filler material addition at eight evenly distributed, opposite positions around the circumference. Execute first from the exterior boundary of the connection, then finalize from the internal boundary. Use welding rod diameter $\emptyset$ 1 mm.
2. **Root Position Exterior (Root 1):** Weld in precise segments. Start at designated position 1 using filler metal rod $\emptyset$ 1 mm. Following completion of the pass segment, allow to cool down naturally until the workpiece and surrounding heat-affected seam area drops below $T < 30^\circ	ext{C}$.
3. **Root Position Interior (Root 2):** Weld in segments. Critical rule: **Rotate the starting point offset by exactly 45°** compared to the starting location of Root 1. Use filler wire $\emptyset$ 1 mm. Allow to cool down to $T < 30^\circ	ext{C}$.
4. **Second Layer Interior (Layer 3):** Weld with addition wire diameter $\emptyset$ 1.2 mm. **Offset the start segment position by an additional 45°** relative to the beginning of Root 2. Allow to cool down to $T < 30^\circ	ext{C}$.
5. **Second Layer Exterior (Layer 4):** Finalize the cover pass with filler wire diameter $\emptyset$ 1.2 mm. Rotate the segment weld start point an additional 45° relative to the starting location of Layer 3.
